It's your responsibility to know and follow these rules!



Bill of Rights

  1. Thou shall enjoy freedom of speech.So long as speech is lawful, it is allowed. There are no other restrictions on statements or discussion. You are free to discuss any topic, including unlawful activity, so long as the purpose is not to facilitate unlawful activity on this forum. See examples of unlawful speech.

  2. Thou shall enjoy freedom of commerce.So long as a transaction is lawful, it is allowed. There are no other restrictions on the types of goods and services that can be offered, and there are no other restrictions on the systems of payment, currency or value exchange that may be used.


Bill of Wrongs

  1. Good Manners Thou shalt not bully, harass, threaten or engage in bigotry. You are allowed to express your honest opinion about any subject being discussed, just don't attack other members with personal attacks.

  2. Illegal Activity Thou shalt not use the forum to facilitate unlawful activity.

  3. Personal Information Thou shalt not post personally identifiable information of any other user.

  4. Account Sharing Thou shalt not lend, rent, sell or otherwise share an Ogrr account.

  5. Profanity in names Thou shalt not create usernames or guilds containing profanity.

  6. URL shortening services Thou shalt not post links produced by url shortening services.1

  7. Begging Thou shalt not beg for donations.2

  8. Multiple Accounts Thou shalt not create several accounts. (We will set-up a system allowing users to post anonymously / with a aliases in the future)

  9. Impersonate Thou shalt not impersonate another user or person.

  10. Advertising Thou shalt not advertise to any external site that has not been expressly authorized by a user in the Ogrr team. This rules applies everywhere

  11. Spam Thou shalt not spam.


Interpretation of rule violations is up to the Ogrr Staff.
If something is borderline, think twice about what you are about to post.


Be sure to read the Trading Rules before using the marketplace.



1The exception to url shorteners is those services that also own the destination URL.
For example: http://goo.gl/maps/2WhT(belongs to Google Maps) and http://youtu.be/GUl9_5kK9ts (belongs to YouTube).
2 Raising funds for a charitable and worthy cause is permissible. Requesting donations for some contribution you've made or service you've performed is permissible.
